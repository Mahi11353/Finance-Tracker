Getting started

How Spendora works

A quick guide to setting up your account, logging expenses, and reading your dashboard — everything you need to take control of your finances.

What is Spendora?

◈

Your personal expense tracker

Spendora is a free, privacy-first expense tracker designed for everyday use. Log your spending, categorise expenses, and instantly see where your money goes — all without spreadsheets, subscriptions, or setup complexity. Every rupee you spend gets recorded, categorised, and visualised so you can make smarter decisions about your money.

Step 1 — Create your account

1

Go to the registration page

Click Create free account on the homepage or use the Get started link in the top navigation bar.

2

Enter your details

Provide your full name, a valid email address, and a password with at least 8 characters. No phone number or credit card required.

3

Account created instantly

Your account is ready right away. You'll be redirected to sign in, then straight to your personal dashboard.

Step 2 — Sign in

1

Go to the sign in page

Click Sign in in the navbar to reach the login form.

2

Enter your credentials

Type in the email address and password you registered with. Your session stays secure — you remain signed in until you explicitly sign out.

3

Land on your dashboard

Signed-in users are taken to their personal dashboard showing all expenses, summary stats, and spending breakdowns.

Step 3 — Add an expense

1

Click "Add Expense" in the navbar

The Add Expense link is always visible in the navigation bar when you're signed in.

2

Fill in the expense details

Enter the amount (in ₹), select a category — Food, Transport, Bills, Health, Entertainment, Shopping, or Other — pick a date, and optionally add a short description.

3

Save and view it instantly

Click Save expense. You're redirected back to your dashboard where the entry appears immediately and all totals update.

Step 4 — Edit or delete expenses

1

Find the expense in your transaction list

Your dashboard shows a full table of recorded expenses — date, description, category badge, and amount on each row.

2

Use the Edit or Delete action

Each row has an Edit link and a Delete button. Edit opens a pre-filled form; delete removes the record permanently.

3

All totals recalculate automatically

After any edit or deletion, your balance, total spent, and category breakdown are updated immediately on the dashboard.

Your dashboard at a glance

₹

Summary stats

Total spent, current balance, and transaction count — updated in real time as you add or remove expenses.

◎

Category breakdown

A visual bar chart shows how your spending splits across each category, with percentages calculated automatically.

◷

Date filters

Filter by date range using quick presets (This month, Last 3 months, Last 6 months) or enter a custom start and end date.

◈

Transaction history

Full list of all expenses in newest-first order, with category badges and descriptions for easy scanning.

Expense tracking tips

📅

Log daily, not weekly

Add expenses the moment they happen so nothing falls through the cracks. The habit that matters most is speed.

🏷️

Use categories consistently

Pick a category and stick with it for similar expenses. Consistent categorisation makes your breakdown charts meaningful.

📝

Add short descriptions

Two words like "Zomato dinner" or "Auto fare" make your transaction list far easier to scan a month later.

📊

Review monthly

At month-end, use the "This month" filter and check which category surprised you — that's usually where the leaks are.

Security & privacy

Your data is safe with Spendora

Your password is never stored in plain text — it is hashed with a modern cryptographic algorithm before being saved to the database. Your expense data is completely private; no other user can see your records. Spendora does not sell or share your data with any third parties. Sessions are protected with server-side tokens, and every sensitive form submission is guarded against cross-site request forgery (CSRF). Your financial data stays on the server you trust — not distributed across third-party clouds.
